7. FFT (Fast Fourier Transformation)

In opposition to autocorrelation, the input signal is now analyzed in the frequency domain - keyword: spectrum.

In the frequency domain, you can see all the frequencies that are involved in the formation of a sound (of the instrument). As mentioned in the previous chapter, there are additional overtones alongside the actual keynotes in guitar or ukulele strings, which account for the typical sound of a string instrument. For simplicity, we will display the low E string (82.4 Hz) once again - here is the spectral view of it:

The relevant peak (82.4 Hz) is the first and quite wide one, followed by another peak, which represents the first overtone.

Finally, the spectral view of a pure sine wave (440 Hz, concert pitch):

In opposition to guitar or ukulele strings, the FFT view now shows a unique maximum - here the frequency can be clearly attributed to the x-axis. As a general rule, however, the higher the measured frequency, the further the appropriate maximum will move to the right.
