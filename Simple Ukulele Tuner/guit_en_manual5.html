5. Calibration

In special circumstances (for example playing in an orchestra) you want to calibrate your instrument(s) lower or higher than the common concert pitch (A, 440 Hz). When playing together with fixed-pitch instruments such as the piano (or oboe), the orchestra will generally tune to them - for example the New York Philharmonic use A = 442 Hz. The pitch of a xylophone - for example - is usually affected by changes in temperature.

You see that the standard pitch (440.0 Hz) stands out with a different font color!

As soon as you have tuned up/down, the new pitch stands out also in the header of the main screen:

In this example, you have a positive offset of about 2.0 Hz - thus your guitar strings must be tuned 2.0 Hz above. Let's take the A-String: instead of 110.0 Hz, you must tune it up to 112.0 Hz in order to satisfy the new pitch of about 442 Hz (thus with the offset of 2.0 Hz).

Warning

There are some limits concerning the pitch range which is between 430 Hz and 500 Hz. If you calibrated below 430 Hz, you would get into trouble with the detection of lower frequencies.
